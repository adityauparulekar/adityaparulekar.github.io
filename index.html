Aditya Parulekar

I am a second year PhD student advised by Prof. Eric Price at UT Austin. Before my Ph.D., I also did my B.S. in Computer Science and Mathematics at UT Austin, with a minor in Sanskrit.

I work on theoretical machine learning, and have been recently thinking about problems relating to the theory of diffusion models and large language models.

Research

  • Diffusion Posterior Sampling is Computationally Intractable

    Shivam Gupta, Ajil Jalal, Aditya Parulekar, Eric Price, Zhiyang Xun

    A lower bound showing that posterior sampling is computationally intractable via diffusion, even for distributions that can be sampled unconditionally.

  • Improved Sample Complexity Bounds for Diffusion Model Training

    Shivam Gupta, Aditya Parulekar, Eric Price, Zhiyang Xun

    Bounds on the sample complexity of training score models that are provably efficient.

  • L1 Regression with Lewis Weights Subsampling

    Aditya Parulekar, Advait Parulekar, Eric Price

    Active sampling for least absolute deviations regression using importance sampling.
